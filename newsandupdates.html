Public Health Alert: Monkeypox Symptoms and Guidance

Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Headache
  • Exhaustion
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Muscle pain
  • Skin rash

If You Have Monkeypox

Please follow this guidance to prevent further spread:

  • Stay at home
  • Avoid contact with people and animals
  • Avoid sharing items
  • Cover your lesions and wear a mask
  • Clean your hands
  • Keep your environment clean
  • Seek medical care

Comparison of Monkeypox, Chickenpox, and Measles

Feature Monkeypox Chickenpox Measles
Fever 1-3 days before rash 1-2 days before rash 3-5 days before rash
Rash appearance Lesions often in one stage of development Lesions often in multiple stages of development Lesions often in multiple stages of development
Rash development Slow Rapid Rapid
Rash distribution More dense on face; present on palms and soles More dense on trunk; absent on palms and soles Starts on face and spreads, sometimes reaching hands and feet
Lymphadenopathy Present Absent Occasional
Death Up to 10% Rare Varies widely

If you suspect you have Monkeypox, please contact us or your nearest health provider immediately for guidance.
